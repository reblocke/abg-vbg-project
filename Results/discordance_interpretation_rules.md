# Discordance Interpretation Rules

These rules intentionally separate broad workflow/completeness signals from stronger evidence for modality-specific procedure-capture bias.

- Reference-profile / standardization is supported when common-reference or common-source marginal standardization shrinks at least one current probability gap by at least 25%.
- Setting/workflow heterogeneity is supported when within-modality setting ranges exceed 10 percentage points or IMV-focused within-setting/site diagnostics show persistent heterogeneity.
- Differential missingness or procedure capture is supported only when at least two enhanced proxy families cross the 5-point threshold and event-conditional timestamp completeness also points to a difference; otherwise any weaker signal is partial.
- Sentinel procedure capture/completeness is supported only when at least two selected low/medium-specificity sentinels show large differences in the same direction; workflow-dependent sentinels are partial evidence.
- Timing is inconclusive unless feasible gas/procedure pairs can be parsed; timing-restricted differences above 5 points are treated as partial evidence, not proof.
- Tail support is supported when the existing ESS/event-count support flag is triggered.
- Intrinsic modality-specific measurement is not supported by default when dual-tested summaries are available for review.
